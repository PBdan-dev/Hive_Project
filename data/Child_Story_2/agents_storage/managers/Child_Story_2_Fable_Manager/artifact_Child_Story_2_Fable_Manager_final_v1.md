### 📜 FINALIZED DELIVERABLE: ITERATION v1

**Title:** The Wooden Heart of Elara

Elara ran her thumb over Pip’s smooth honeywood feet. The last stroke of sandpaper dust settled as twilight brushed the workshop window. Pip’s river-glass eyes caught the dying sunbeam—and blinked.

The village hearth-lantern sputtered and died. Winter winds howled beyond the door. Pip stepped down, his crimson joint-threads humming faintly. *“The Star-Shard sleeps in the Ancient Oak,”* he chimed, voice like dry leaves. *“Without it, the frost will take our warmth.”*

Elara packed a satchel. Together, they slipped into the Whispering Woods.

At Glimmer Creek, rushing water swallowed the stepping stones. Pip leapt, slipped, and nearly tumbled under. He paused, drew a luminous crimson thread from his wrist, and wove it between two mossy rocks. A trapped frog hopped onto his shoulder, nudging the thread tighter. They crossed safely. *“Patience bridges what force breaks,”* Pip tapped softly.

Deeper in, Tanglewood Hollow choked the path. Thick brambles locked Pip’s joints. Fear made him stiffen. Remembering Elara’s quiet hums back home, he slowed his breathing. His threads relaxed. He mimicked a soft wind-call, calming the twisting vines, and slipped through a hidden gap. *“Breathe,”* he reminded himself. *“Even wood knows how to bend.”*

They found the Ancient Oak hollowed by time. Inside lay the Star-Shard—cold, dull, and dormant. It refused to wake. Pip knelt, pressed his palms to the crystal, and carefully chipped away a sliver of his own chest-wood. He cradled the shard in the hollow space. Warmth bloomed. The crystal blazed gold, singing with captured starlight. Pip grew heavy, his movements slowing as energy drained. But the light was safe.

The return journey was swift. Elara carried Pip when his joints grew stiff, sharing stories until his crimson threads brightened once more. At the village square, she placed the shard into the hearth-lantern. Golden fire roared to life, chasing the frost from cobblestones and windows alike. Families cheered. Pip stood quietly, his wood now holding a permanent, gentle glow.

Back in the workshop, Elara set him on the shelf. She smiled, tracing the fresh scar on his chest.

*“Magic isn’t carved into wood or whispered in spells,”* she whispered. *“It wakes inside us when we choose courage over fear, and kindness over convenience.”*

Pip’s glass eyes reflected the lantern’s warmth. Silent, still, and whole.
