Here is a tightly scoped, chapter-by-chapter breakdown designed for a concise yet compelling French YA fantasy novella/novel (~8 chapters). Each entry includes a narrative milestone and a structural turning point, calibrated to reveal the uncommon lore organically while centering the protagonist’s psychological arc.

---

### **CHAPITRE 1 : Le Fil Cassé**
- **Milestone:** Introduction of the protagonist (Sylve), her anomalous ability (*la Rupture d’Écho*: she can extract, reverse, or erase memory-resonances trapped in objects/places), and the setting’s core mechanic: reality is sustained by woven echoes of forgotten moments. Hints of her dark past surface through fragmented visions.
- **Turning Point:** An Echo-Inquisitor corners her using a resonance snare. In panic, Sylve unspools a nearby artifact, accidentally releasing a buried coordinate pointing to the *Cité des Ombles* (the ruined heart of her past). She must flee before the local reality destabilizes.

### **CHAPITRE 2 : Les Ruines de l’Oubli**
- **Milestone:** Journey begins across landscapes shaped by collapsed memory-timelines. Sylve meets Kael, a rogue archivist who trades in orphaned echoes. Worldbuilding expands: society hoards echoes as currency, law, and architectural mortar.
- **Turning Point:** Their supposed safehouse is a hunter’s trap. Kael initially intends to hand her over, but witnesses Sylve stabilize a decaying echo-zone instead of destroying it. He switches sides, forging a fragile alliance.

### **CHAPITRE 3 : La Voix du Verre**
- **Milestone:** Sylve practices controlling her power. Flashbacks reveal the truth behind her backstory: she was the primary vessel for an order that harvested children’s memories to “preserve” a dying city. She wasn’t merely a victim; she consented.
- **Turning Point:** Ambushed by harvesters, Sylve fully activates her power, erasing a foundational echo. The resulting reality fracture injures Kael. She realizes her ability doesn’t just destroy—it rewrites causal threads, but exacts a toll: each rupture costs her a personal memory.

### **CHAPITRE 4 : L’Encre Noire**
- **Milestone:** They infiltrate a subterranean archive where echoes are bound into glass-texts. Sylve uncovers records proving the “preservation” ritual was a cover to cage a primordial force known as *le Silence*, which naturally resets overloaded realities.
- **Turning Point:** Touching a sealed text containing her own erased childhood memory, Sylve experiences a full vision: she chose the vessel role to prevent total annihilation. Her guilt transforms into purpose. Hunters breach the archive; escape triggers a high-speed chase.

### **CHAPITRE 5 : Le Pont des Soupirs**
- **Milestone:** Traversal of a bridge maintained by collective sorrow-echoes. Sylve must carefully modulate her power to avoid collapse while evading pursuit. Kael provides tactical support and emotional grounding.
- **Turning Point:** A hunter detonates a resonance charge mid-crossing. To save others, Sylve absorbs the blast into herself. She survives but permanently loses a core memory (her mother’s name/face). Emotional nadir; doubt creeps in.

### **CHAPITRE 6 : Le Cœur du Résonateur**
- **Milestone:** Arrival at the epicenter of the original collapse. Discovery: *le Silence* isn’t a monster but a natural corrective wave. The order’s attempt to freeze time caused stagnation, decay, and the very hunters now pursuing her.
- **Turning Point:** Confrontation with the former Grand Weaver, now a hollow echo-wraith. He offers Sylve restoration of her memories and dominion over a stabilized world. She refuses, recognizing that forced permanence is spiritual death. Prepares to trigger a controlled unspooling.

### **CHAPITRE 7 : La Rupture**
- **Milestone:** Climax. Sylve channels her power to shatter the central anchor binding the false timeline. Reality fractures and reforms: echoes materialize visibly, gravity shifts, memories bleed into the environment. Allies hold off enforcers.
- **Turning Point:** Tempted to erase all pain, Sylve instead weaves her trauma into the new world’s foundation. The old structure dissolves; a raw, untamed but living reality emerges. The wraith is released/freed. Sylve accepts her scars as integral, not defective.

### **CHAPITRE 8 : L’Aube des Échos Libres**
- **Milestone:** Aftermath. Sylve and companions navigate the reborn world where magic flows openly rather than being hoarded. Society begins adapting to fluid reality. Sylve stops running.
- **Turning Point:** Final image: Sylve hears an unfamiliar echo in the wind—a reminder that stories continue beyond her. She steps forward not as a fugitive or savior, but as a conscious weaver of what comes next. Satisfying, open-ended closure fitting YA tone.

---

### **Execution Notes for Scope & Tone**
- **Pacing:** 8 chapters keeps the narrative lean. Each chapter advances plot, deepens lore, and pushes character development simultaneously.
- **Lore Integration:** Unconventional magic system (memory/echo resonance) is revealed through action, not exposition. Rules are consistent: echoes sustain reality, extraction causes decay, reversal risks fragmentation.
- **Backstory Handling:** Dark past is unpacked gradually across Ch 1–4, culminating in agency rather than victimhood. Fits YA themes of self-forgiveness and reclaimed identity.
- **Power Mechanics:** “Weird” ability is narratively functional: it solves problems, creates consequences, and mirrors internal growth. Costs (memory loss) raise stakes without melodrama.
- **Language Note:** The final manuscript will be written in French, preserving poetic cadence appropriate for YA fantasy while maintaining tight prose to respect the “just enough” constraint.
