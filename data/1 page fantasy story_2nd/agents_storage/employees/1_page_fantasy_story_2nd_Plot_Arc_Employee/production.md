### THREE-ACT STRUCTURE MAP
*(Optimized for a ~60–70k YA fantasy novel; scoped tightly to maintain focus and pacing)*

---

#### **ACT I: SETUP & INCITING INCIDENT** (~25%)
- **Opening Image**: Lyra (17) works as a “memory-sweeper” in a border settlement, scrubbing residual emotional echoes from abandoned buildings. She isolates herself, suppressing flashbacks of the “Silence Plague” that destroyed her hometown years ago.
- **World & Lore Introduction**: Magic operates via *Resonance Weaving*—reality is sustained by “Chords,” vibrational frequencies born from collective emotion and memory. The world fractured centuries ago into the *Shattered Chords*. Weavers manipulate these frequencies, but raw exposure causes psychological decay. Society fears “Echo-Touched” individuals like Lyra.
- **Inciting Incident**: A sealed artifact—the *Primal Shard*—arrives at her workshop. When Lyra touches it, it violently resonates with her nervous system, projecting a vision: a looming *Dissonance Wave* that will unravel local reality unless anchored by a living conduit.
- **Call → Refusal → Threshold**: An exiled Weaver tracks her down, revealing the Plague was a covered-up ritual failure, not her fault. He urges her to seek the *First Note* (a harmonic anchor point). Lyra refuses, terrified of repeating history. The Shard activates autonomously, dragging her into a memory-echo of the fracture event. She crosses the threshold, leaving town with the mentor.

---

#### **ACT II: CONFRONTATION & RISING ACTION** (~50%)
- **Tests & Allies**: Lyra trains in controlled resonance. Gains two companions: a pragmatic cartographer who maps echo-zones, and a defected Council Enforcer seeking redemption. Group dynamics form around trust vs. fear of her power.
- **Midpoint**: At an ancient archive, Lyra touches a relic and experiences a full memory-echo of the original fracture. Discovers her power (*Echo-Siphoning*) doesn’t destroy—it absorbs and restructures traumatic frequencies. The “dark backstory” was engineered blame; she was a scapegoat. Goal shifts from survival to active restoration.
- **Complications**: The Weaver Council dispatches hunters to seize the Shard. During skirmishes, Lyra’s power destabilizes under stress, causing localized reality glitches (time stutters, gravity shifts, phantom voices). Paranoia fractures the trio. Guilt resurfaces.
- **All Is Lost / Dark Night of the Soul**: Ambushed at a resonance nexus. Mentor falls protecting her. Lyra panics, siphons too deeply, and accidentally erases a companion’s recent memories. Believes she’s inherently destructive. Retreats alone into a dead-zone. Confronts her trauma head-on: realizes suppression amplifies the damage; integration and intentional resonance is the only path forward. Reclaims agency.

---

#### **ACT III: CLIMAX & RESOLUTION** (~25%)
- **CLIMAX**: Lyra returns to the *Fracture Spire*, epicenter of the incoming Dissonance Wave. Faces the Council’s champion and raw chaotic resonance. Instead of fighting, she opens herself completely, siphoning the dissonant frequencies. Her power peaks: she weaves the shattered echoes into harmony, risking total cognitive dissolution. Success hinges on accepting her pain rather than fleeing it. The wave stabilizes; reality knits back together.
- **FALLING ACTION**: Survivors regain memories peacefully. The Council’s authoritarian grip collapses as truth surfaces. Companions reconcile; lost memories return organically through stabilized resonance.
- **RESOLUTION**: Lyra stops hiding. Establishes a sanctuary for Echo-Touched youth, teaching controlled resonance as healing, not weaponry. Final image mirrors the opening: she stands in a quiet room, not scrubbing away echoes, but listening to them. Tone: grounded, hopeful, emotionally precise. Scope remains tight—no empire-building, no sprawling prophecies. Focus stays on identity, trauma integration, and responsible power.

---

#### **STRUCTURAL NOTES FOR EXECUTION**
- **Pacing**: Lean and deliberate. Each act advances both external plot (stabilizing the Chords) and internal arc (guilt → acceptance → purpose). Subplots are minimal; every scene serves theme or progression.
- **Power Mechanics**: *Echo-Siphoning* scales with emotional honesty. Overuse = fragmentation; controlled use = restoration. Tied directly to character growth.
- **Lore Differentiation**: Magic is psycho-acoustic, not elemental or mana-based. Conflict stems from societal fear of vulnerability, not good vs. evil binaries.
- **YA Alignment**: Themes of self-worth, found family, and reclaiming narrative ownership. Stakes remain personal and immediate. Prose-ready for French adaptation with lyrical, restrained tone.
